# Skill: /campaign-brief

Generate a marketing campaign brief for a product launch, promotion, or brand initiative.

## Usage

```
/campaign-brief <campaign-name> [--launch|--promo|--brand|--nurture] [--b2b|--b2c]
```

- `<campaign-name>` — Short name for the campaign, e.g. `spring-sale`, `product-x-launch`, `enterprise-awareness`.
- `--launch` — New product or feature launch campaign.
- `--promo` — Time-limited promotional campaign (discount, bundle, event).
- `--brand` — Brand awareness or thought leadership campaign.
- `--nurture` — Lead nurture or customer retention email/content series.
- `--b2b` — Business audience (longer cycle, stakeholder-focused).
- `--b2c` — Consumer audience (shorter cycle, emotion/value-driven).

---

## Before drafting

Ask the user for:
1. Campaign objective (1 sentence: what does success look like?).
2. Primary target audience (persona, segment, or job title).
3. Key message or offer (what are we saying or giving?).
4. Budget range and available channels.
5. Campaign dates (start, end, key milestones).
6. Any existing brand guidelines, positioning docs, or past campaigns to reference.

## After generating

Remind the team to:
- Get sign-off from stakeholders on the objective and key message before briefing creative.
- Set up UTM parameters for all paid and email links before launch.
- Schedule a post-campaign debrief within 2 weeks of end date.
